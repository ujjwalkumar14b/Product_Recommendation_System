Collaborative Filtering
Collaborative filtering recommends products based on the behavior and preferences of similar users. If multiple users liked the same products, the system predicts that they may like similar products in the future as well.

This method focuses on user interaction data such as ratings, clicks, or purchases rather than product details. It is widely used in platforms like Amazon and Netflix for recommending product and movies respectively.
Content Based Filtering
Content-based filtering recommends products similar to items a user has previously shown interest in. It analyzes product attributes like category, descriptions and features to generate personalized recommendations.

For example, if a user purchases gaming laptops, the system may recommend other laptops with comparable hardware specifications and processing power. It help users to discover relevant products that closely match their interests.
Hybrid Recommendation System
Hybrid filtering combines collaborative and content-based recommendation techniques to improve overall accuracy and relevance. By leveraging the strengths of both methods, it minimizes individual limitations and generates more reliable, diverse, and personalized recommendations for users.

By analyzing user behavior alongside product characteristics, the system delivers highly relevant recommendations, enhancing user experience, engagement, satisfaction, and overall shopping outcomes.
Popularity Based Filtering
Popularity-based filtering recommends products that are trending, highly rated or frequently purchased across the platform. These recommendations are not personalized but help users discover widely preferred products and improve engagement, especially among first-time visitors.

This technique is particularly useful when user interaction data is limited or unavailable. During the cold start problem, it provide relevant suggestions before sufficient behavioral information has been collected.
